SECTION 1: YOUR PAY AND PAYROLL

1.1 Pay Schedule
- Employees are paid bi-weekly (every two weeks) on Fridays
- 2025 payroll calendar available on the HR Portal (hr.company.com)
- Direct deposit is mandatory — paper checks issued only in exceptional cases

1.2 Viewing Your Pay Stub
- Access pay stubs via the Employee Self-Service Portal (ess.company.com)
- Stubs available 2 business days before pay date
- Includes: gross pay, deductions, taxes withheld, net pay, and YTD totals

1.3 Payroll Deductions
Standard deductions from your paycheck:
- Federal, state, and local income taxes (per your W-4/state forms)
- Social Security (6.2%) and Medicare (1.45%)
- Health, dental, vision insurance premiums
- 401(k) / retirement contributions (voluntary)
- Flexible Spending Account (FSA) contributions

1.4 Pay Corrections
- Payroll errors reported to HR within 10 days of pay date
- Corrections processed in the next payroll cycle
- Off-cycle payments approved only for errors above $500

SECTION 2: EXPENSE MANAGEMENT

2.1 Finance Portal Overview
All expense claims submitted through the Finance Portal (finance.company.com):
- Log in with your company SSO credentials
- Create new expense report → add line items → attach receipts → submit for approval
- Status tracked in real-time; email notifications at each stage

2.2 Expense Submission Deadlines
- Standard expenses: Submit within 30 days of the expense date
- Travel expenses: Submit within 10 business days of return
- Year-end (December): All expenses submitted by December 20

2.3 Receipt Requirements
- Digital receipts accepted (PDF, JPG, PNG)
- Required for all expenses above $25
- Itemized receipts required for all meals
- Hotel folios required (showing room rate and itemized charges)

2.4 Common Expense Categories
- Meals & Entertainment: Business meals, team lunches, client dinners
- Travel: Flights, hotels, ground transport, parking
- Office Supplies: Items for home office or in-office use
- Professional Development: Training, conferences, books, certifications
- Communications: Business phone, internet, subscriptions
- Client Gifts: Gifts to clients (see Gift Policy limits)

SECTION 3: CORPORATE CARDS AND SPENDING TOOLS

3.1 Corporate Credit Card
- Available to employees with regular business travel or purchasing needs
- Apply via Finance Portal with manager approval
- Monthly statements reconciled within 5 business days
- Spend limits: $5,000/month standard; higher limits require VP approval

3.2 Petty Cash
- Petty cash funds available in select offices for incidental expenses under $50
- Petty cash custodian logs all disbursements; monthly reconciliation required
- Department heads accountable for petty cash fund in their area

3.3 Expense Advance
- Available for anticipated expenses above $500
- Request via Finance Portal with business justification
- Reconciled within 15 days of incurring the expense
- Unspent advances returned to Finance promptly

SECTION 4: BUDGET AND FINANCIAL PLANNING

4.1 Annual Budget Process
- Budget cycle runs October–November for the following fiscal year
- Department heads submit budget proposals via the Budget Planning Tool
- Finance consolidates and presents to leadership for approval by December 15
- Approved budgets communicated to departments by January 15

4.2 Budget Monitoring
- Department heads receive monthly Budget vs. Actual reports from Finance
- Finance Dashboard available for real-time spend tracking (dashboard.finance.company.com)
- Variances above 15% require written explanation to Finance Director

4.3 Budget Transfers
- Transfers within the same cost center: Department head approval
- Transfers between cost centers: Finance Director approval
- Emergency budget increases: CFO approval with business case

SECTION 5: FINANCIAL CONTROLS AND COMPLIANCE

5.1 Segregation of Duties
- No single employee can both approve and process a financial transaction
- Finance staff rotate responsibilities periodically to maintain integrity
- Managers cannot approve their own expense claims

5.2 Fraud Prevention
- Report suspected fraud immediately to the Ethics Hotline (ethics@company.com or ext. 4000)
- Anonymous reporting available — no retaliation for good-faith reports
- Finance conducts periodic internal audits of expense reports and vendor payments

5.3 Conflict of Interest
- Employees must disclose any personal relationships with vendors or clients
- Employees involved in a vendor selection cannot have financial interest in that vendor
- Annual Conflict of Interest Declaration required from all employees

5.4 Data Privacy in Financial Transactions
- Financial data classified as Confidential — share only on need-to-know basis
- Never email sensitive financial information unencrypted
- Payment card information (PCI data) handled only by authorized Finance staff
