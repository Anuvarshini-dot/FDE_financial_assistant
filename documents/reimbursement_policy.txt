EMPLOYEE REIMBURSEMENT POLICY
Version: 3.2 | Effective Date: January 1, 2025 | Approved by: CFO

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
This policy governs reimbursement of expenses incurred by employees in the course of conducting
company business. It applies to all full-time, part-time, and contract employees across all departments.

2. GENERAL REIMBURSEMENT LIMITS

2.1 Meal Expenses
- Breakfast: Up to $20 per person
- Lunch: Up to $35 per person
- Dinner: Up to $60 per person
- Daily meal cap (all meals combined): $100 per day
- Client entertainment meals: Up to $150 per person (requires manager pre-approval)

2.2 Local Transportation
- Taxi/rideshare (Uber, Lyft): Reimbursed at actual cost with receipt
- Personal vehicle mileage: $0.67 per mile (IRS standard rate, updated annually)
- Parking fees: Reimbursed at actual cost, up to $30 per day in metro areas
- Public transportation: Reimbursed at actual cost with receipt

2.3 Communication Expenses
- Mobile phone business use: Up to $50 per month
- Internet for remote work: Up to $60 per month with documented business need
- International roaming charges: Up to $100 per trip with prior approval

3. APPROVAL THRESHOLDS
- Expenses up to $500: Direct manager approval required
- Expenses $500–$2,000: Department head approval required
- Expenses above $2,000: CFO or Finance Director approval required
- Emergency expenses above $5,000: CEO approval required

4. SUBMISSION REQUIREMENTS
- All reimbursement claims must be submitted within 30 calendar days of the expense date
- Late submissions (31–60 days) require department head justification
- Claims older than 60 days will not be reimbursed without CFO exception
- Original receipts are required for all expenses above $25
- Itemized receipts required for all meal expenses regardless of amount
- Electronic receipts are accepted in PDF or image format

5. NON-REIMBURSABLE EXPENSES
The following expenses will NOT be reimbursed:
- Personal entertainment, gym memberships, or personal subscriptions
- Fines, penalties, or traffic violations
- Alcohol purchased without client entertainment context
- Spouse or family member travel expenses
- Expenses related to personal days taken during business trips
- Luxury upgrades (business class without approval, luxury hotels above policy rate)
- Lost or stolen cash or personal items
- Expenses without supporting receipts above $25

6. REIMBURSEMENT PROCESS
Step 1: Employee submits expense claim via the Finance Portal (finance.company.com)
Step 2: Supporting receipts uploaded as attachments
Step 3: Direct manager receives notification for approval
Step 4: Finance team reviews within 5 business days
Step 5: Approved amounts processed in the next bi-weekly payroll cycle
Step 6: Employee receives email confirmation with payment details

7. SPECIAL CIRCUMSTANCES
- Home office setup: One-time allowance of $500 for remote employees (approved by HR and Finance)
- Professional development: Up to $1,500 per year per employee (separate Education Policy applies)
- Relocation expenses: Covered under separate Relocation Policy

8. DISPUTES AND EXCEPTIONS
Disputed reimbursements should be raised with the Finance team within 10 business days of the
decision. Exceptions to this policy require written approval from the CFO. All exceptions are logged
and reviewed quarterly by the Finance Governance Committee.
