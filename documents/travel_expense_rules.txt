CORPORATE TRAVEL EXPENSE POLICY
Version: 2.8 | Effective Date: January 1, 2025 | Approved by: VP Finance & HR

1. POLICY OVERVIEW
This policy defines standards for business travel expenses, booking procedures, and reimbursement
for all employees traveling on company business. All travel must have a clear business purpose.

2. BOOKING REQUIREMENTS
- All domestic travel must be booked through the approved Corporate Travel Portal (travel.company.com)
- International travel requires Travel Request Form (TRF) submitted 10 business days in advance
- Last-minute travel (< 48 hours notice) requires written VP-level approval
- Personal bookings without portal use may result in reduced reimbursement

3. AIRFARE
3.1 Domestic Flights
- Economy/coach class is mandatory for all domestic flights
- Flights under 6 hours: Economy only, no upgrade reimbursement
- Preferred booking: 14+ days in advance to secure best fares
- Maximum reimbursable fare: Lowest available economy fare at time of booking approval

3.2 International Flights
- Flights over 8 hours: Business class permitted with VP approval
- Flights 6–8 hours: Premium economy permitted with director-level approval
- Flights under 6 hours: Economy class only
- Stopovers for personal reasons: Only economy-equivalent cost is reimbursed

3.3 Baggage
- One checked bag reimbursed per trip (up to $35 each way)
- Additional bags require justification (e.g., equipment transport)

4. HOTEL ACCOMMODATION
4.1 Domestic Travel
- Standard hotel rate limit: $200 per night (excluding taxes and fees) in non-metro areas
- Metro areas (NYC, SF, LA, Chicago, Boston): Up to $275 per night
- Hotel selection: Preferred vendors via Corporate Travel Portal get priority
- Stay duration: Only nights directly adjacent to business meeting days

4.2 International Travel
- Rate limits vary by destination — refer to the Country Rate Table in the Travel Portal
- Default cap: $250 USD equivalent per night
- Luxury hotels (5-star) require CFO approval regardless of rate

4.3 Extended Stays
- Stays exceeding 7 consecutive nights: Finance team must review alternatives (serviced apartments)
- Weekly apartment rentals may be arranged for assignments longer than 2 weeks

5. GROUND TRANSPORTATION
- Airport transfers: Taxi, rideshare, or shuttle reimbursed at actual cost
- Rental cars: Economy/compact class only; insurance through corporate account
- Personal vehicle for business travel: $0.67 per mile IRS rate
- Tolls and parking: Reimbursed at actual cost with receipts

6. PER DIEM RATES
Per diem is an alternative to itemized meal receipts for qualifying travel:
- Domestic travel per diem: $75 per day
- International travel per diem (standard): $120 per day
- High-cost international cities (London, Tokyo, Zurich, Oslo): $160 per day
- Per diem is reduced proportionally for partial travel days

7. INTERNATIONAL TRAVEL REQUIREMENTS
- Travel pre-approval form required 10 business days before departure
- Travel insurance arranged through HR — mandatory for all international trips
- Visa and passport fees are reimbursed as part of travel expenses
- Foreign currency exchange fees reimbursed at actual cost
- International expenses should be submitted in local currency with exchange rate noted
- Security briefing required for travel to high-risk regions (as designated by the Security team)

8. COMBINING PERSONAL AND BUSINESS TRAVEL
- Only the business portion of combined travel is reimbursable
- If personal extension adds cost to return flights, the difference is borne by the employee
- Accommodation during personal days is NOT reimbursable
- A written declaration form must be submitted for any personal day extensions

9. TRAVEL ADVANCES
- Travel advances available for trips exceeding $1,000 in estimated expenses
- Advance requests submitted 5 business days before travel
- Reconciliation required within 15 days of return; unused advances returned to Finance

10. EXPENSE REPORTING FOR TRAVEL
- Travel expense reports due within 10 business days of return
- All receipts must be attached — hotel folios, airline itineraries, meal receipts
- Group meals: Itemized receipt + list of attendees and business purpose required
