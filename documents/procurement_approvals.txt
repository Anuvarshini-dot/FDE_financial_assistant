PROCUREMENT APPROVAL POLICY & PURCHASING GUIDELINES
Version: 3.0 | Effective Date: January 1, 2025 | Approved by: CFO & COO

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
This policy governs the procurement of goods and services by all company departments. It defines
approval authorities, required documentation, and purchasing thresholds to ensure fiscal responsibility
and regulatory compliance.

2. PROCUREMENT PRINCIPLES
- All purchases must have a clear, documented business purpose
- Value for money must be demonstrated (competitive quotes for significant purchases)
- Preferred vendors from the Approved Vendor List (AVL) should be prioritized
- Personal purchases using company funds are strictly prohibited
- Purchases must align with the department's approved annual budget

3. APPROVAL THRESHOLDS AND AUTHORITIES

3.1 Goods and Standard Services
- Up to $1,000: Employee self-approval with manager notification (P-card use permitted)
- $1,001–$5,000: Direct manager/supervisor approval required
- $5,001–$25,000: Department Head/Director approval + 3 competitive quotes required
- $25,001–$100,000: VP approval + formal RFQ/RFP process + Finance review
- $100,001–$500,000: CFO approval + formal RFP + Legal contract review
- Above $500,000: Board/Executive Committee approval required

3.2 IT and Technology Purchases
- All IT purchases regardless of amount: IT Director pre-approval required for compatibility
- Software licenses: IT Security review mandatory for cloud/SaaS tools
- Hardware: IT Asset Management team coordinates all hardware procurement
- IT-related purchases above $10,000: CTO approval in addition to financial thresholds

3.3 Professional Services (Consultants, Lawyers, Agencies)
- Up to $10,000: Department Head approval
- $10,001–$50,000: VP + Finance approval, SOW required
- Above $50,000: CFO approval + Legal review of engagement contract

4. REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

4.1 Purchase Requisition (PR)
Required for all purchases above $1,000. Must include:
- Detailed description of goods/services needed
- Business justification
- Budget line item reference
- Estimated cost and preferred vendor (if applicable)
- Required delivery date

4.2 Purchase Order (PO)
Issued by Finance/Procurement after PR approval. Required for:
- All purchases above $2,500
- All recurring vendor relationships
- All purchases with delivery lead time > 7 days

4.3 Competitive Quotes
Required based on value:
- $5,000–$25,000: Minimum 3 written quotes from different vendors
- Above $25,000: Formal Request for Quotation (RFQ) or Request for Proposal (RFP) process
- Sole-source justification form required if competitive quotes not obtained
- Quote comparison documented on Vendor Selection Form (VSF)

4.4 Contracts
Required for:
- Any engagement above $10,000
- Recurring services regardless of amount
- Any agreement with IP, data privacy, or liability implications
- All contracts reviewed by Legal if above $50,000 or contain non-standard terms

5. PROCUREMENT PROCESS FLOW
Step 1: Need identification and budget check
Step 2: Submit Purchase Requisition via Procurement Portal (procure.company.com)
Step 3: Manager approval in system
Step 4: Finance validates budget availability
Step 5: Procurement team issues RFQ/RFP (if required)
Step 6: Vendor selection and approval
Step 7: Contract negotiation (if applicable)
Step 8: Purchase Order issued
Step 9: Goods/services received and verified
Step 10: Invoice matched to PO — payment initiated

6. CORPORATE PURCHASE CARD (P-CARD) POLICY
- P-Cards issued to employees with frequent small purchasing needs (subject to VP approval)
- Single transaction limit: $1,000
- Monthly spending limit: $5,000 (standard); up to $10,000 with VP approval
- Prohibited P-Card purchases: Cash advances, personal items, travel (use Travel Portal), gift cards
- Monthly reconciliation required within 5 business days of statement close
- Receipts required for all transactions above $25
- Misuse results in immediate card revocation and potential disciplinary action

7. EMERGENCY PURCHASES
- Genuine operational emergencies: VP-level authorization by phone/email permitted
- Emergency purchases above $10,000: CFO verbal approval + written confirmation within 24 hours
- Retroactive PO creation required within 2 business days for emergency purchases
- Frequency of emergency purchases tracked — excessive use triggers process review

8. PROHIBITED PURCHASING PRACTICES
- Splitting purchases to avoid approval thresholds (purchase splitting) — grounds for termination
- Purchasing from vendors with undisclosed personal relationships (conflict of interest)
- Committing company funds without proper authorization
- Accepting kickbacks, gifts above $50, or improper benefits from vendors
- Using personal funds expecting reimbursement for controlled purchases (above $500)

9. BUDGET COMPLIANCE
- All purchase requests validated against approved department budget
- Purchases exceeding budget require budget amendment approval from Finance
- Departments notified monthly of budget consumption via Finance Dashboard
- Year-end purchase cutoff: December 15 for current fiscal year expense recognition
