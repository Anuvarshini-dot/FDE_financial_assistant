TAX COMPLIANCE GUIDELINES FOR EMPLOYEES AND DEPARTMENTS
Version: 2.5 | Effective Date: January 1, 2025 | Approved by: CFO & Tax Counsel

1. PURPOSE
These guidelines ensure that all employees, departments, and contractors comply with applicable
federal, state, and local tax laws, and internal tax reporting obligations.

2. EMPLOYEE TAX DOCUMENTATION

2.1 New Employee Requirements
All new employees must submit within their first week:
- Completed W-4 (Employee Withholding Certificate) — updated version required
- State tax withholding form (varies by work state)
- Direct deposit authorization form
- I-9 Employment Eligibility Verification

2.2 Annual Tax Updates
- W-2 forms issued by January 31 each year for prior tax year
- 1099 forms issued to contractors receiving $600+ in the prior year
- Employees should review W-4 annually, especially after life events (marriage, new dependent)

3. CONTRACTOR AND FREELANCER TAX RULES
- Independent contractors paid $600+ in a calendar year receive IRS Form 1099-NEC
- Contractors must submit W-9 before first payment
- Non-US contractors submit W-8BEN (individuals) or W-8BEN-E (entities)
- Backup withholding (24%) applies if contractor fails to provide valid TIN
- Contractors are NOT employees — no benefits, payroll taxes, or workers' compensation apply

4. BUSINESS EXPENSE TAX IMPLICATIONS
4.1 Taxable vs. Non-Taxable Reimbursements
Non-taxable (not included in income):
- Business travel expenses (accountable plan reimbursements with receipts)
- Meals with documented business purpose
- Professional development under qualified education assistance ($5,250 limit/year)
- De minimis fringe benefits (occasional small perks)

Taxable (included in W-2 income):
- Personal use of company vehicles (must be reported)
- Moving expense reimbursements (post-2017 tax reform)
- Employer-paid athletic club memberships
- Cash gifts or bonuses above $25 per occasion
- Reimbursements without proper documentation (non-accountable plan)

4.2 Gift and Entertainment Tax Rules
- Business gifts to clients: Deductible up to $25 per recipient per year
- Meals with clients (50% deductible): Require business purpose documentation
- Entertainment expenses: Generally NOT deductible under current tax law
- Employee recognition gifts: Tax-free up to $400 per year (tangible property only)

5. SALES TAX COMPLIANCE
- Purchases must include applicable sales tax unless a valid exemption certificate is on file
- Tax-exempt purchases require a completed Exemption Certificate (Form ST-120 or state equivalent)
- Departments must NOT request vendors to omit sales tax without a valid exemption
- Finance maintains the official list of tax-exempt states and categories

6. PAYROLL TAX OBLIGATIONS
- FICA taxes (Social Security 6.2% + Medicare 1.45%) withheld from all employee wages
- Additional Medicare tax (0.9%) withheld for wages exceeding $200,000
- Federal income tax withheld based on W-4 elections
- State and local income taxes withheld per employee's work location
- Employer portion of FICA matched by company

7. INTERNATIONAL TAX CONSIDERATIONS
7.1 Foreign Travel and Per Diem
- IRS per diem rates for foreign travel published annually — refer to IRS Publication 1542
- Excess per diem above IRS rates is taxable income

7.2 Expatriate and Cross-Border Employees
- Employees on international assignments must consult with Tax team before relocation
- Tax equalization agreements available for long-term international assignments
- Foreign tax credits may apply — contact Tax team for individual analysis
- Employees working remotely from foreign countries may create tax nexus — must notify HR/Tax

7.3 Transfer Pricing
- All intercompany transactions must comply with arm's-length transfer pricing rules
- Transfer pricing documentation maintained by Tax team
- Cross-border service agreements require Tax team review

8. TAX AUDIT SUPPORT
- Employees must retain business expense records for minimum 7 years
- Tax team must be immediately notified of any tax authority inquiries or audits
- Do NOT respond to tax authority notices without consulting the Tax team
- All subpoenas or legal notices forwarded to Legal and Tax teams immediately

9. REPORTABLE TRANSACTIONS
Employees must report to the Tax team:
- Any transaction exceeding $10,000 in cash (Bank Secrecy Act)
- Foreign bank accounts with balances exceeding $10,000 at any point in the year (FBAR requirement)
- Significant unusual transactions that may have tax implications
