VENDOR PAYMENT PROCEDURES & ACCOUNTS PAYABLE POLICY
Version: 4.1 | Effective Date: January 1, 2025 | Approved by: CFO & Legal

1. PURPOSE
This document outlines the procedures for processing vendor payments, managing accounts payable,
and ensuring compliance with corporate financial controls and procurement standards.

2. VENDOR ONBOARDING REQUIREMENTS
Before any payment can be processed, vendors must be registered in the Vendor Management System (VMS):

Required Documentation:
- Completed Vendor Registration Form (VRF-01)
- W-9 form (domestic vendors) or W-8BEN (international vendors)
- Certificate of Insurance (COI) — minimum $1M general liability
- Bank account details via secure ACH Authorization Form
- Signed Vendor Code of Conduct agreement
- Business license or registration certificate

Verification Process:
- Finance team verifies vendor details within 5 business days
- Legal reviews contracts above $50,000
- New vendors are subject to KYC (Know Your Customer) screening
- Vendors on government sanction lists will not be onboarded

3. INVOICE SUBMISSION REQUIREMENTS
All invoices must include:
- Vendor name, address, and tax ID
- Invoice number (unique per vendor)
- Invoice date and payment due date
- Itemized description of goods or services
- Purchase Order (PO) number (mandatory for PO-based transactions)
- Payment terms (e.g., Net 30, Net 60)
- Bank details or payment instructions

Submission Methods:
- Electronic: invoices@company.com (preferred)
- Vendor Portal: vendors.company.com
- Physical mail: Finance Department, 3rd Floor

4. PAYMENT APPROVAL WORKFLOW
4.1 Purchase Order (PO) Based Payments
Step 1: Invoice received and matched to PO in accounting system (3-way match: PO + receipt + invoice)
Step 2: Discrepancies flagged to Procurement and vendor within 2 business days
Step 3: Matched invoices auto-approved for payment per payment terms
Step 4: Payment batch processed on Tuesday and Thursday of each week

4.2 Non-PO Payments
Step 1: Department submits Payment Request Form (PRF) with business justification
Step 2: Approval thresholds apply:
  - Up to $5,000: Department Manager approval
  - $5,001–$25,000: VP/Director approval
  - $25,001–$100,000: CFO approval
  - Above $100,000: CFO + CEO approval
Step 3: Finance validates vendor registration
Step 4: Payment processed within 5 business days of full approval

5. PAYMENT TERMS AND METHODS
5.1 Standard Payment Terms
- Default terms: Net 30 (payment within 30 days of invoice date)
- Early payment discount: 2/10 Net 30 (2% discount if paid within 10 days)
- Extended terms (Net 60, Net 90): Require CFO approval and contract clause

5.2 Payment Methods
- ACH/Bank Transfer: Preferred for all domestic vendors (no fee)
- Wire Transfer: For international vendors or urgent payments (bank fees apply)
- Corporate Check: Issued for vendors without ACH setup (additional 3-day processing)
- Corporate Credit Card: For approved recurring subscriptions under $5,000/month
- Cash payments: NOT permitted under any circumstances

6. INTERNATIONAL VENDOR PAYMENTS
- All international payments processed via wire transfer
- Currency: Paid in contract currency; exchange rate applied on payment date
- Additional documentation: Vendor's bank SWIFT code and IBAN required
- Regulatory: OFAC screening performed before every international payment
- Processing time: 5–7 business days from approval
- Wire fees: Charged to requesting department's cost center

7. DISPUTE RESOLUTION
- Invoice disputes must be raised within 15 days of invoice receipt
- Finance logs dispute in accounts payable system, notifying vendor within 2 business days
- Target resolution: 10 business days for domestic, 15 for international disputes
- Partial payments permitted for undisputed portions of invoices

8. VENDOR PAYMENT HOLDS
Payments may be placed on hold for:
- Missing or incorrect vendor documentation
- Invoice discrepancies not resolved
- Active legal or compliance investigation
- Vendor on government sanctions list
- Breach of Vendor Code of Conduct

Vendors notified of hold within 2 business days. Finance Director approval required to lift holds.

9. RECORD KEEPING
- All payment records retained for 7 years per regulatory requirements
- Electronic records stored in the Finance Document Management System (FDMS)
- Physical records stored in the Finance Archive Room (access restricted)
